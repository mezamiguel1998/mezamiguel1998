JonBenet Ramsey Case: Boulder Police Respond to Unearthed DNA Bombshell

The Boulder Police Department has confirmed that they are aware of a report claiming that new DNA evidence in the cold case murder of JonBenet Ramsey is a "game changer."

The six-year-old beauty queen was found dead in the basement of her family's Boulder, Colorado, home on December 26, 1996. The mysterious murder has remained unsolved for more than two decades, despite global attention and numerous investigations.

In recent days, a local news outlet cited a source claiming that newly discovered DNA evidence could help solve the infamous case. However, the Boulder Police Department is urging caution regarding the report.

A spokesperson for the department released a statement saying, "We are aware of the recent reporting, and it is not accurate. We have not received any new DNA evidence in the Ramsey case."

As the case remains unsolved, those involved in the investigation continue to search for answers in hopes of bringing closure to this tragic and perplexing case.
